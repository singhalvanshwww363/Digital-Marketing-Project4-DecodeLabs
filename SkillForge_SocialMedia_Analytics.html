Vanity Metrics vs. Business Metrics

❌ Vanity Metrics (Stop Tracking)

  • Total Followers (doesn't = revenue)
  • Total Likes (feels good, means little)
  • Total Impressions (no context)
  • Post Count (quantity ≠ quality)

✅ Business Metrics (Track These)

  • Engagement Rate (% of audience engaging)
  • Click-Through Rate (traffic intent)
  • Conversion Rate (leads generated)
  • Cost Per Lead (budget efficiency)

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Every metric must answer one question: Does this data help us make a better decision? If not, it is a vanity metric. Track only what you can act on.

The 4 Metric Categories

📡

Reach Metrics

How many people saw your content. Impressions, Reach, Follower Growth Rate.

💬

Engagement Metrics

How people interacted. Likes, Comments, Shares, Saves, Engagement Rate %.

🔗

Traffic Metrics

Click-Through Rate, Link Clicks, Website Sessions from Social.

💰

Conversion Metrics

Leads Generated, Enrolments, Cost Per Lead, ROI on Content.

The Social Media Analytics Funnel

Analytics must map to the buyer journey. Every metric sits at a specific funnel stage. Knowing WHERE a metric lives tells you WHAT to do with it.
🔍 AWARENESS — Reach, Impressions, Follower Growth
▼
💬 ENGAGEMENT — Likes, Comments, Shares, Saves, Engagement Rate
▼
🔗 CONSIDERATION — Link Clicks, Profile Visits, Story Swipe-Ups
▼
💰 CONVERSION — DM Enquiries, Sign-ups, Enrolments, Revenue

Insight 2 — Audience Behaviour Patterns

  • Peak activity hours: LinkedIn 7–9am weekdays · Instagram 6–9pm weekdays & Saturday noon
  • Save behaviour: Carousel posts on "career tips" and "Python tutorials" get 3x more saves — bookmark intent shows high value content
  • Comment triggers: Posts ending with a question get 4x more comments than posts without a CTA
  • Share triggers: Data-backed posts (with statistics) get 2.5x more shares than opinion posts
